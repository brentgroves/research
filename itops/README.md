# IT Operations

IT Operations (ITOps) is the management of an organization's daily IT services, infrastructure (networks, servers, cloud), security, and support to ensure systems run reliably, securely, and efficiently, keeping business functions running smoothly and supporting growth. It's the core function that manages the how behind the tech, handling maintenance, incident response, user access, and implementing new tech to keep everything functional and aligned with business goals.
Key Responsibilities
Infrastructure Management: Overseeing servers, networks, storage, and cloud environments.
System Monitoring: Keeping an eye on performance to prevent issues.
Incident Response: Quickly fixing problems when they arise to minimize downtime.
Security Management: Protecting systems and data from threats.
User Support: Ensuring employees and users can access the tools they need.
Business Continuity: Planning for disasters and data recovery.
Compliance & Governance: Ensuring adherence to policies and regulations.
Why It's Important
Reliability: Prevents costly downtime and ensures continuous service.
Efficiency: Optimizes processes, often through automation, to reduce manual work.
Scalability: Provides a stable foundation for new technologies and business growth.
Security: Protects valuable data and systems from breaches.
ITOps vs. Other IT Functions
ITIL Framework: ITOps is one of four key areas in the IT Infrastructure Library (ITIL), alongside Technical Management, Application Management, and the Service Desk.
DevOps: While DevOps focuses on fast software delivery, ITOps concentrates on the stable, ongoing management and maintenance of the underlying systems that support those applications.
